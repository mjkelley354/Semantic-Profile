About Me

My name is Michael Kelley and I'm an IT Professional with 15+ years experience in Banking Operations, Banking IT, Professional Services and Software Engineering. As a Product Manager, I act as an advocate for Systems Management software, develop new feature MVPs, set work prioritization and minimize the cost to bring new products and features to market but most importantly, I strive to understand the frustrations and needs of my customers and work to build new features and products to meet those requirements.

I've been a techie sort of guy since grade school but originally chose a different career and education path. After a few post-college years in the working world, I returned to college in a technical program, got my first job at a help desk in a bank and continued my career path in Banking IT and arrived at my current position with a major software and hardware vendor for the banking and retail industries.

Working in (and around) Software Engineering has peeked my curiosity about how software applications work and are constructed. While I've taken a number of classes over the years in Visual Basic, Fortran, database technologies, Java and so on, I decided, in 2018 to dive a bit deeper and attend a Full Stack coding boot camp with Georgia Tech.

My current interest are in HTML5, Javascript, CSS and Node but I continue to rely on old favorites like SQL, shell scripting and Java (and occasionally VB) for daily tasks. The boot camp experience has introduced me to a number of newer technologies all of which I'm deeply curious about but the most intriguing of which is Node js.
